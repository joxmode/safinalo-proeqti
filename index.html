MF DOOM

The masked villian of underground hip-hop

don't forget all caps when you spell my name...

MF DOOM (born Daniel Dumile) was one of underground hip-hop’s most influential and enigmatic figures, celebrated for his intricate, multisyllabic rhyme schemes and brilliant storytelling. After a tragic early career setback in the group KMD involving the death of his brother, Dumile re-emerged in the late 1990s as a comic-book-inspired supervillain, adopting a permanent, gladiator-style metal mask to force audiences to focus entirely on his music rather than his image. Operating through brilliant alter-egos like King Geedorah and Viktor Vaughn, he reached a creative peak with Madvillainy (2004)—a collaborative masterpiece with producer Madlib that remains universally regarded as one of the greatest rap albums of all time. Before his passing in 2020, "your favorite rapper's favorite rapper" completely redefined independent music, leaving behind a legendary legacy built on abstract jazz samples, lyrical complexity, and complete artistic anonymity.
